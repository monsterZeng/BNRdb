About

Denitrification is an essential microbial process of reducing nitrate and nitrite to gaseous forms of nitrogen, chiefly nitrous oxide (N2O), and nitrogen (N2). The ability to denitrify is widely dispersed among prokaryotes. It can be viewed as either beneficial or detrimental, depending on the objectives. From removing excess nitrogen in the ecosystem, reducing the chances of eutrophication and to the production of nitrous oxide gas as a by-product of the process is deemed detrimental to the environment as nitrous oxide is a potent greenhouse gas.

MicDenitrDb database is a novel manually curated integrative resource concerned with microbial denitrification, serving as a user-friendly access point for the utilization and querying of a catalog of experimentally validated genetic and biochemical information of denitrifying microorganisms. Denitrification associated genomic datasets are essential for improving bioreactor system stability, nitrogen removal efficiencies, and setting up of optimal biotechnological and environment parameters in nitrogen removal. The database incorporates a set of utilities, enabling interactive data browsing, sequence homology searches, multiple sequence alignments, and phylogeny exploration. Based on the information obtained from the MicDenitrDb, it will be possible to select the most suitable microbial denitrifiers that can ultimately reduce nitrate to nitrogen. For instance, the addition of an appropriate archaeon to biological wastewater treatment systems will result in effective nitrogen removal processes. MicDenitrDb is a reference guide facilitating gene mining and the creation of genetic toolboxes for predictive microbial ecological studies of denitrifying microbes. Studying functional microbial guilds helps researchers understand microbial ecology and encourage the development of effective biodegradation technologies that ensure high performance in environmental remediation.
